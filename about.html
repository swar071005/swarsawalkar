I am Swar Sawalkar, a seeker of Indian classical music, where melody meets discipline and emotion finds its voice. Music, for me, is not an act of performance, but a lifelong path of listening, learning, and evolving through every sur and sam.

üé∂ Early Musical Influence

From the very beginning of my musical journey, I have closely followed and absorbed the styles of legendary Indian classical and light music exponents. I grew up listening to maestros such as Bharat Ratna Pt. Bhimsen Joshi, Pt. Jitendra Abhisheki, Swar Kokila Lata Mangeshkar, and Asha Bhosle and many others.

Listening to these great artists inspired me to observe their voice culture, phrasing, emotional depth, and discipline. I would often attempt to imitate their styles and renditions as a learning exercise, helping me develop sensitivity towards sur, laya, bhava, and clarity in singing.

üéº Early Training, Listening & Musical Journey

My journey in music began at home. I was first introduced to music by my mother, who herself is a music teacher. Observing my inclination and interest in music from an early age, she encouraged me to pursue formal training and took me to Pt. Smt. Vidya Jail, a respected disciple of Sangeet Samragini Begum Parveen Sultana.

Under her guidance, I received a strong foundation in Hindustani classical music, focusing on riyaz, voice culture, and emotional expression. During this phase, I received several opportunities to perform on various platforms, including devotional venues and cultural programs. I was also featured on television channels such as Zee TV and Star Plus, which gave me early exposure to professional performance environments.

Through a reference from Mrs. Sreerupa Ghosh, who has been my coordinator for several years, I was introduced to the professional music industry. Under her guidance, I was launched into voice-over and dubbing work, allowing me to explore music beyond stage performances and gain experience in studio-based vocal work.

As my musical journey progressed, I began advanced training under the guidance of Mahesh Kale, a renowned Hindustani classical vocalist and disciple of Pt. Jitendra Abhisheki. Under his mentorship, I continue to deepen my understanding of classical aesthetics, gayaki, and expressive nuance, and I remain a dedicated student to this day.

üéº Listening & Musical Taste

My favourite playback singers include Kishore Kumar and Mohammed Rafi, whose versatility, tonal balance, and emotive storytelling continue to inspire me deeply. I also explore works of several other legendary singers to understand vocal dynamics across genres.
